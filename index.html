What is RAM used for?

When you open a program such as Microsoft Word, the computer loads the application into
its RAM. When you open a document that you already have saved on your computer,
the operating system finds the file in long-term storage and copies the information
into its RAM. These are used for everything on your computer, I mean EVERYTHING.

Are there different types of RAM?

The currently most popular type of RAM is DDR-SDRAM, a synchronous dynamic random access memory
with twice the data transfer rate. DDR (double data rate) allows multiple files to be transferred
at the same time. The latest version of DDR5 RAM can achieve a data transfer speed of about 6.4
gigabytes per second.However, supply chain bottlenecks make obtaining DDR5 memory difficult and
very expensive. The most commonly used RAM remains DDR4, which also comes in many types and speeds.
Typically, these memory cards operate at speeds of 2400 MHz or higher. Video RAM is even faster
than regular memory and typically comes in the form of GDDR (Graphics Double Data Rate),
a special type of memory optimized for graphics playback. The latest generation – GDDR6 – can
achieve data transfer speeds of 16 Gbit/s.

How much memory do I need?

The more RAM your computer has, the faster it runs. If your device is old, you might need
to upgrade the RAM or other hardware. Every open application (including browser tabs)
consumes RAM. When you run out of RAM, your computer has to move things around to free
up space on the hard disk, which slows down your computer. The amount of RAM you need
depends on the apps and programs you use, how many of them you have open at the same time,
and how impatient you are.

How much RAM do you need?

Generally, users recommend 8GB of RAM for casual computer usage and internet browsing,
16GB for spreadsheets and other office programs, and at least 32GB for gamers and
multimedia creators. How you use your computer influences how much RAM you need,
so use this as a guideline.
